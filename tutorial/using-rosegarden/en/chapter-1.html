1 Introduction

1.1 What is Rosegarden?

Rosegarden-4, hereafter referred to as simply Rosegarden, is a versatile application that seeks to be a useful tool for those wanting to create music. It combines aspects of a MIDI sequencer, an audio sequencer, and a notation editor into one convenient, easy to use package that provides users with a consistent and intuitive interface.

Rosegarden is a native KDE application that can run on any system that has the KDE and QT libraries installed, and currently requires a minimum of KDE/QT 3.1 in order to run. Many of its features may only work Linux systems, but it is designed to compile and run on any Unix-like platform capable of running KDE. (See the FAQ for details on building for systems that can't run ALSA.)

1.2 This Guide's Raison d'être

My goal in writing this guide is to help you explore Rosegarden, and to discover ways to use its features to solve real problems that you might encounter when creating your own music. Rosegarden is easy to use, once you come to understand its framework, but it's not quite like anything you are likely to have used in the past. As such, I wish to take you on a guided tour through Rosegarden, to demonstrate many of its capabilities, and to offer you a foundation from which your own creative projects can spring.

1.3 Version Requirements

This document describes Rosegarden4, the latest in this venerable line of applications. As of this writing, we're at version 0.9.9_cvs. The latest official release is 0.9.8. There are myriad older versions floating around, and some features described here may not work unless you're running something very recent.

1.4 The Author's Studio

Obviously, what I can actually demonstrate is hardware dependent. I'd like you to bear in mind what is sitting in front of me as I write this, and thus I will share the details of my "studio."

I'm running a 2.0 GHz P4 with 512 MB of RAM; Debian Sarge, and KDE 3.2.3. I've compiled Debian's kerenel 2.4.26 source with Debian's pre-empt and low latency patches.

My MIDI landscape centers around an SB Live! with an old joystick MIDI port box from Gravis. I have the MIDI out from a Fatar MIDI-72 controller keyboard attached to the MIDI in on the box, and the MIDI out on the box connects to the MIDI in on my Roland SC-33 Sound Canvas. I usually load PC51f.sf2 into the SB Live!, and rarely change it.

In the way of other gear, I have a reasonably good electret microphone from Radio Shack, which I use to record my acoustic guitar, flute, and other traditional instruments. I have an electric guitar and a DigiTech RP-200 effects box for those times I want to play rock star.

I route all of this through my Sound Blaster's mixer. I can't currently have everything feeding into the soundcard at the same time, and I must swap cables around in order to feed either the Sound Canvas or the RP-200 into the "line in" jack.

1.6 Getting This Document

The latest version of the Using Rosegarden is always available at http://rosegarden.sourceforge.net/tutorial/using-rosegarden/

You can also get it from CVS . It's part of Rosegarden, but it's a separate module, so you don't get it unless you specifically check out the tutorial module.

cvs -Q -z3 -d:pserver:anonymous@cvs.rosegarden.sourceforge.net:/cvsroot/rosegarden checkout tutorial

Remember that anonymous CVS at SourceForge is still lagging behind, so updates might take a day or two to filter to the mirrors.
