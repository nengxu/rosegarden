About the authors

Rosegarden-4 has three core developers, who between them are to blame for almost exactly 99% of the code currently in the application. You can help to reduce this figure by contributing to the development effort yourself.

Chris Cannam

Chris is an unemployed good-for-nothing, wasting his days while his girlfriend scrapes in enough money to keep their small baby in nappies and formula. He lives in London.

He first started working on Rosegarden at the end of 1993. It was then a university project for two students, devised by professor John Fitch at the University of Bath, with Andy Green working on the MIDI sequencer and Chris on the notation editor. They were the sole developers until late in 1995, some time after the barely-usable 1.0 release. For Rosegarden-4, Chris is responsible for most of the notation rendering and some of the file and data code. He also made this website.

Richard Bown

Richard is a software developer, musician and producer living in London. He's been a Rosegarden developer since the end of 1995, when he was drafted in to take over the Unix sequencer. He is responsible for almost all the MIDI, audio, sequencing and recording code in Rosegarden-4.

Richard recently took a serious amount of time out to better understand the practical side of music software and computer based music production in the home. Results are one very bad album, one very good no-nonsense web-guide to home recording and a much improved knowledge of the MIDI and audio concerns faced by the Rosegarden team. He aims to eventually use Rosegarden for all future production work.

Guillaume Laurent

Guillaume lives in Cannes, in the South of France. (Lucky Guillaume.) He's been using Linux since 1995, and first worked on Rosegarden in the summer of 1996, when he supplied a few hundred lines of new code in his first couple of emails. Guillaume is responsible for getting the Rosegarden-4 project restarted, for creating the basic design and writing much of the GUI code and file and data framework, and for most of the project administration.

He has also helped maintain Gtk-- for a while, and is involved in some parts of KDE here and there. In his spare time, he works as a software engineer.
